Geunjeongjeon 01

The main hall of Gyeongbokgung Palace, the royal palace of Joseon. In Jeongjeon, officials greeted the king with New Year's greetings, held state ceremonies, and welcomed foreign envoys.

During the Joseon Dynasty, it was used as a space for the king's life and administrative affairs, and important ceremonies and events were also held here. Geunjeongjeon is the central building of the Joseon Dynasty and is known for its splendid skylights and delicate decorations. The structure of the building is single-story and has a characteristic roof shape, a hipped roof.

The inside of Geunjeongjeon is decorated with beautiful wooden architecture, and in the center there is a ghost gate through which sunlight and moonlight enter.
In addition, cultural heritages of the Joseon Dynasty, such as furniture used in the court, calligraphy and painting, and antiques, are exhibited.

Gyeonghoeru 02

It was used as a place for kings of the Joseon Dynasty to discuss important matters with their subjects and to hold banquets on national occasions.

It consists of a first floor with a wide floor in front and a corridor above it. On the first floor, there is a space where the king sits and discusses with the audience or manages the realm. This space was mainly used for important ceremonies and national events of the Joseon Dynasty, the king's praise and award ceremonies, and receptions of foreign envoys.

It follows traditional wooden architecture and is decorated with beautiful carvings and decorations. The interior is richly decorated, symbolizing the king's feats and its role as a venue for important ceremonies.

Hyangwonjeong 03

It was used as a living space for women of the Joseon Dynasty, and has the meaning that the scent spreads far.

Hyangwonjeong Pavilion was used as a living space for women of the Joseon Dynasty, where women spent leisurely time enjoying cultural activities, reading, and enjoying their hobbies. The queens received etiquette education here, and also engaged in artistic activities such as literature, music, and art. Hyangwonjeong Pavilion was a space that supported women during the Joseon Dynasty to carry out their roles and duties and engage in creative activities.

Palace of the Joseon Dynasty,
Traditional Korean Architecture

Gyeongbokgung Palace is a representative building of Korean architecture. It follows the typical hanok architectural style and consists of beautiful wooden buildings. The palace buildings are built with traditional wooden structures, mainly oak and pine for pillars and beams. And the buildings harmonize with the manicured gardens to create a beautiful landscape.
